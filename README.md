# Adrien Abecassis

I work on international policy, global governance, and the institutional response to large-scale collective risks.

My professional background spans the French diplomatic service, the Élysée Palace, Harvard University, UCLA, and the Paris Peace Forum. Across these roles, I have worked on European affairs, technology governance, climate policy, development finance, and global public goods.

This GitHub account is a working space for projects at the intersection of policy, research, data, and software. It includes prototypes, reading tools, public-policy experiments, and technical explorations linked to my broader work.

## Main areas of work

### Artificial intelligence and digital governance

I work on the governance of digital technologies, with a focus on AI, digital rights, institutional coordination, and the distribution of technological benefits and risks across societies.

### Climate overshoot and planetary risks

I work on climate overshoot governance, including mitigation, adaptation, carbon removal, solar radiation modification, and the institutional trade-offs involved in managing these options together.

### Democratic governance and public opinion

I have worked on democratic legitimacy, public opinion, institutional trust, and the changing relationship between citizens, institutions, and information systems.

### Global public goods

Much of my work concerns cooperation problems that exceed the capacity of single governments: climate, health, digital infrastructure, development finance, and emerging technologies.

## Professional background

I am a career French diplomat by training.

I served at the Élysée Palace from 2012 to 2017, working on European affairs, policy analysis, and public opinion. I later held fellowships at Harvard and UCLA, where my research focused on international affairs, technology policy, climate governance, and environmental law.

I currently lead policy initiatives at the Paris Peace Forum, where I work on governance projects related to global public goods, including AI, climate, health, digital policy, and development finance. In 2023, I served as Deputy Special Envoy for the Summit for a New Global Financing Pact.

## Purpose of this GitHub

This is a policy workshop rather than a conventional software-engineering portfolio.

The code here is used to test ideas, structure information, build small tools, and explore how computation can support public reasoning. Some projects are polished. Others are experimental.
